How to Wedge

  1. Prepare your workspace:
    • Find a clean, sturdy table or workbench to wedge the clay.
    • Use a non-porous surface like canvas or a plaster bat to work on.
  2. Get your tools ready:
    • Wire cutter or clay cutting tool
    • Rolling pin
    • Spray bottle filled with water (optional)
  3. Cut the clay:
    • Take a block of clay and slice off a manageable portion using a wire cutter or clay cutting tool.
    • Aim for a chunk that is roughly one-fourth to one-third of the total clay mass.
  4. Slam the clay:
    • Hold the chunk of clay with both hands and forcefully throw it down onto your work surface.
    • Repeat this process several times, flipping the clay and slamming it down again.
  5. Cut and stack:
    • Cut the slammed clay in half, creating two equal-sized pieces.
    • Place one piece on top of the other, rotating it 90 degrees.
  6. Slam and repeat:
    • Once again, slam the stacked clay forcefully onto the work surface.
    • Repeat the process of cutting, stacking, and slamming 10-15 times or until the clay feels consistent.
  7. Wedge the clay:
    • Form the clay into a cone shape.
    • Hold the tip of the cone with one hand and use the other hand to push down and wedge the clay by rolling it forward and backward.
    • Apply steady pressure as you roll the clay from the tip to the wider base, gradually working your way down the cone.
    • Repeat this process several times until the clay is well-wedged and has a uniform texture.
  8. Check for consistency:
    • Slice a section of the clay using a wire cutter to inspect the cut surface.
    • Look for any air pockets or inconsistencies.
    • If needed, repeat the wedging process to remove any remaining air bubbles.
