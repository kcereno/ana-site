How to Center Clay

  1. Prepare your wheel:
    • Ensure the pottery wheel is clean and ready for use.
    • Adjust the height and position of the wheel for comfortable access.
  2. Prepare your clay:
    • Take a portion of clay suitable for your project.
    • Ensure it is a manageable size for effective centering.
  3. Prepare the wheel head:
    • Wet the surface of the wheel head or bat slightly for lubrication.
  4. Position the clay:
    • Place the clay in the center of the wheel head, pressing it down gently.
  5. Start the wheel:
    • Turn on the pottery wheel at a slow to medium speed.
    • Adjust the speed as desired for comfort and control.
  6. Brace the clay:
    • Wet your hands slightly and position your palms firmly on top of the clay to create a dome shape.
    • Apply gentle pressure to anchor the clay to the wheel head.
  7. Start centering:
    • With your left hand supporting the clay from the side and your right hand from above, apply equal pressure with both hands.
  8. Establish a cone shape:
    • Press the clay downward and inward, gradually creating a cone shape.
    • Apply symmetric pressure to keep the clay balanced.
  9. Continue centering:
    • Maintain steady pressure and periodically wet your hands to keep the clay moist.
    • Smooth out any irregularities and refine the shape by working up and down the cone.
  10. Check for centering:
    • Occasionally stop the wheel to assess the centeredness of the clay.
    • Look for wobbling or unevenness.
    • If necessary, continue the centering process until the clay is balanced and spins uniformly.
  11. Adjust and repeat:
    • If the clay is off-center, gently shift your hands to apply pressure to the uncentered area and correct its position.
    • Repeat the centering process as needed until the clay is properly centered.
