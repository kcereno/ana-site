How to Reclaim

  1. Collect the dry clay:
    • Gather all the dry or hardened clay scraps or pieces that you want to reclaim.
  2. Break the clay into small pieces:
    • Use your hands or a clay cutter to break the dry clay into small, manageable pieces.
  3. Place the clay in a container:
    • Transfer the broken clay pieces into a plastic or ceramic container with a lid.
    • Ensure the container is clean and free of any contaminants.
  4. Add water to the container:
    • Pour water into the container, covering the clay pieces completely.
    • The amount of water needed will depend on the quantity of clay being reclaimed.
  5. Allow the clay to soak:
    • Let the clay soak in the water for several hours or overnight.
    • The water will gradually seep into the clay, softening and breaking it down.
  6. Break up the clay further:
    • After the clay has soaked, use your hands or a wooden tool to further break up the softened clay.
    • This will help ensure an even consistency throughout.
  7. Mix and knead the clay:
    • Start kneading and mixing the clay, incorporating any remaining dry spots with the softened clay.
    • This process helps distribute the moisture evenly and homogenizes the clay's texture.
  8. Test the clay's consistency:
    • Take a small portion of the reclaimed clay and perform a pinch test.
    • Squeeze the clay between your fingers to check if it holds together without cracking or crumbling excessively.
    • If the clay feels too dry, add a little more water and continue kneading.
  9. Optional drying and wedging:
    • If the reclaimed clay feels too wet or soft, let it air dry for a while or spread it out on a plaster bat to absorb excess moisture.
    • Once the clay reaches a workable consistency, you can wedge it to further improve its texture and remove any air bubbles.
  10. Store the reclaimed clay:
    • Once the clay has been reclaimed and wedged, store it in an airtight container or wrap it tightly in plastic to prevent it from drying out again.
    • Label the container with the clay type and date of reclamation for future reference.
