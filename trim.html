How to Trim

  1. Set up your workspace:
    • Find a clean, sturdy table or workbench to work on.
    • Ensure you have enough space and good lighting.
    • Place a pottery wheel or a rotating surface (such as a banding wheel) on the table.
  2. Prepare the pottery piece:
    • Place the pottery piece securely on the wheel or rotating surface.
    • Ensure it is centered and stable.
  3. Select your trimming tools:
    • Choose a trimming tool appropriate for the task, such as a trimming loop or a trimming tool with a sharp edge.
    • Make sure the tool is clean and sharp for precise trimming.
  4. Wet the pottery piece:
    • Dampen the surface of the pottery piece with water or use a sponge.
    • This helps to prevent the tool from sticking to the clay and allows for smoother trimming.
  5. Start trimming:
    • Begin by gently pressing the trimming tool against the outer edge of the pottery piece.
    • Slowly move the tool towards the center while maintaining a steady pressure.
    • Remove small amounts of clay at a time to control the trimming process.
  6. Trim the excess clay:
    • Trim away any excess clay from the bottom or sides of the pottery piece.
    • Aim to create a smooth, even contour and remove any uneven or unwanted areas.
    • Take care not to trim too deeply or compromise the structural integrity of the piece.
  7. Refine the shape and details:
    • Pay attention to the desired shape and details of the pottery piece.
    • Use the trimming tool to refine curves, angles, or any specific features.
    • Smooth out rough edges or uneven surfaces.
  8. Check the thickness:
    • During the trimming process, periodically check the thickness of the walls.
    • Use a caliper or your fingers to measure the thickness and ensure consistency.
    • Adjust the trimming as needed to achieve the desired thickness.
  9. Clean and finish:
    • Remove any clay remnants or debris from the trimmed piece.
    • Smooth the trimmed edges and surfaces with a sponge or your fingertips.
    • Pay attention to the overall finish and make any final adjustments.
  10. Allow the piece to dry:
    • Once you have finished trimming, set the pottery piece aside to dry.
    • Follow the recommended drying time for your specific clay type and thickness.
