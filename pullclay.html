How to Pull

  1. Prepare your workspace:
    • Find a clean and sturdy table or workbench to work on.
    • Ensure you have enough space to maneuver comfortably.
    • Place a non-porous surface like canvas or a plaster bat on your work area to prevent the clay from sticking.
  2. Gather your tools:
    • Clay of your choice.
    • Water in a spray bottle or container.
    • Pottery wheel or a flat surface for hand-building.
  3. Center the clay:
    • Start by centering a lump of clay on the pottery wheel or flat surface.
    • Apply gentle pressure to the clay to ensure it is centered and stable.
  4. Wet your hands:
    • Dip your hands in water or use a sponge to moisten them. This helps prevent the clay from sticking to your hands.
  5. Position your hands:
    • With your hands positioned on both sides of the clay, slightly cup them and press them firmly into the center of the clay, creating a depression or well.
  6. Apply even pressure:
    • As the wheel spins or if you're working by hand, gradually apply even pressure with your palms while keeping your fingers relaxed.
    • Gently squeeze the clay between your hands, slowly moving upward.
  7. Gradually lift and pull:
    • As you apply pressure, start to lift and pull the clay upward using a controlled motion.
    • Maintain an even pressure and speed while pulling up the walls.
  8. Maintain thickness and shape:
    • Keep an eye on the thickness of the clay walls to ensure consistency.
    • Adjust your hands' pressure and speed as needed to maintain the desired thickness and shape of the walls.
  9. Use water for lubrication:
    • If the clay begins to resist your pulling motion, moisten your hands or the clay surface with a small amount of water.
    • This helps to lubricate the clay and allows for smoother pulling and shaping.
  10. Repeat and refine:
    • Continue to repeat the process of lifting and pulling up the walls, gradually increasing the height.
    • Take breaks to observe the shape and adjust as needed, refining the form and thickness.
  11. Smooth and shape the rim:
    • Once you've achieved the desired height, use your fingers or a rib tool to smooth and shape the rim of the clay walls.
    • Ensure the rim is even and free from any irregularities.
  12. Trim excess clay (optional):
    • If necessary, trim any excess clay from the top of the walls using a sharp tool or a wire cutter.
